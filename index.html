Before we talk about me – let's talk about revolut.com

A few product-owner-level observations about revolut.com/business – the product this role manages.

Observation 1 · CRO / UX

The /business homepage has competing CTAs that could dilute conversion.

The page surfaces "Open an account", "Speak to Sales", and a promotional trial banner simultaneously. For a visitor who's mid-funnel (comparing plans), these create decision friction. I'd test consolidating to a single primary CTA per scroll section – e.g. leading with "Compare plans" for browsers and reserving "Speak to Sales" for the Enterprise tier – then measure click-through by segment.

Observation 2 · SEO / GEO

Blog content is ageing and not structured for featured snippets or AI overviews.

Key guides like "Spend Management: The Complete Guide" date back to 2022 and use narrative-heavy copy without clear H2/H3 question-answer structure. As Google shifts toward AI Overviews and SGE, content that isn't structured as direct answers to high-intent queries will lose visibility. I'd prioritise a content refresh programme – starting with the top 10 traffic-driving posts – adding FAQ schema, definition boxes, and clear headings that match how people actually search (e.g. "What is spend management?" as an H2).

Observation 3 · Information Architecture

The pricing page buries the plan comparison table below testimonials and feature lists.

Visitors comparing Basic vs Grow vs Scale have to scroll past marketing copy and social proof before reaching the comparison table. This increases bounce risk for high-intent "pricing" keyword traffic. I'd test moving the interactive comparison table above the fold – or at minimum, adding sticky anchor links – so visitors can self-select a plan faster. The FAQ section at the bottom duplicates information already in the table, which could be consolidated.

Case Study 1 · CRO & Experimentation

How One Experiment Generated +$2M

Coles · 2024 · Scaling experimentation across one of Australia's highest-traffic retail websites

The Problem

Content decisions on coles.com.au were largely driven by stakeholder opinion, not data. Category pages featured manually curated product lists – maintained by hand, limited in range, and updated on gut feel. With millions of monthly sessions, even small inefficiencies had outsized revenue impact. But with a large stakeholder group holding competing priorities, getting buy-in for change meant proving the value of experimentation first.

My Approach

  • Framed the problem as a testable hypothesis: would giving customers access to a broader, auto-filtered product range outperform a hand-picked editorial selection?
  • Used Adobe Target to design an A/B test redirecting category page traffic to a dynamically filtered product list – roughly 10× the SKU count of the curated version.
  • Prioritised this test using ROI-based logic: high traffic × high purchase intent page = large potential upside with low implementation cost.
  • Managed stakeholder alignment across Marketing, Merchandising, and Technology – positioning the test as a low-risk experiment rather than a permanent change.

The Outcome

+$2M in incremental revenue from a single experiment – one of 70+ A/B and multivariate tests run that financial year.

The result shifted the internal conversation from "what do we think works?" to "what does the data say?" – building momentum for a broader culture of experimentation across the web team.

What I'd do differently: I'd push for higher traffic allocation earlier in the test cycle. We ran conservative splits to manage stakeholder comfort, which extended the time to reach statistical significance. In hindsight, the opportunity cost of slower learning outweighed the perceived risk.

Case Study 2 · SEO & Information Architecture

Migrating 100+ Websites Without Losing SEO Equity

Mercedes-Benz Australia (via TeamX / Omnicom) · 2022–2023 · Dealer website migration across 100+ sites

The Problem

Mercedes-Benz Australia's dealer network ran on 100+ individual websites built on a legacy platform. The sites were inconsistent in UX, difficult to maintain, and underperforming on organic search. The business needed to migrate every dealer site to a modern web platform – without losing the SEO equity built up over years of indexed URLs, backlinks, and local search rankings.

My Approach

  • Framed the migration as an SEO preservation project first, redesign second. Every URL mapping decision was evaluated against organic traffic risk before UX goals.
  • Defined a unified URL structure and information architecture across all 100+ sites to improve crawlability and create a consistent user journey from search to conversion.
  • Partnered with an external SEO agency to map legacy URLs, implement redirect chains, and align the new content structure with technical SEO best practices.
  • Coordinated cross-functional delivery across design, development, and client stakeholders – authoring creative briefs and scoping each dealer site rollout in phases to manage risk.
  • Ran retailer training and change management sessions to drive adoption across the dealer network.

The Outcome

30% improvement in conversion rate across 100+ dealer websites post-migration. Organic traffic was preserved within 90 days through structured redirect mapping – avoiding the traffic cliff that typically follows migrations of this scale.

What I'd do differently: I'd have built automated 404 monitoring from day one rather than relying on manual crawl audits post-launch. At 100+ sites, broken redirects surfaced slowly and some took weeks to catch.

↳ Revolut Connection

The Revolut PO (Website) role lists "defining, delivering, and optimising information architecture" as responsibility number one. This project is that – at scale. Migrating 100+ customer-facing sites without losing organic search performance required the same discipline Revolut needs: rigorous URL strategy, SEO preservation, and cross-functional delivery across design, engineering, and external partners.

Case Study 3 · Data & CRM Integration

Turning a Brochure Website Into a Lead Engine

Mercedes-Benz Australia & New Zealand (via Publicis Emil) · 2019–2021 · CRM-integrated lead capture across national campaigns

The Problem

Mercedes-Benz ran national marketing campaigns that drove traffic to the website – but the lead capture experience was broken. Forms were static, not optimised for mobile, and disconnected from the CRM. Marketing had no visibility into which website interactions converted to qualified leads. Dealers waited days for lead data to arrive. The business needed the website to function as a lead engine, not just a brochure.

My Approach

  • Designed and deployed mobile-first dynamic forms integrated with Salesforce Marketing Cloud, creating a direct data pipeline from website to CRM.
  • Used Adobe Analytics to identify where users dropped off in the lead capture flow, then redesigned form UX based on behavioural data – shorter forms, smarter field logic, mobile-native layout.
  • Managed the website in Adobe Experience Manager (AEM), defining new component requirements and content governance processes to support rapid campaign launches.
  • Partnered with SEO and marketing stakeholders to optimise campaign landing pages for organic search – reducing reliance on paid traffic to fill the funnel.
  • Established structured WIP cadences across teams to improve delivery speed and reduce the risk of broken integrations at launch.

The Outcome

40% increase in mobile form completion rate after replacing static desktop-era forms with mobile-first dynamic fields integrated with Salesforce Marketing Cloud. Lead-to-CRM sync dropped from days of manual processing to near real-time, giving dealers faster access to qualified leads from national campaigns.

What I'd do differently: I'd have pushed for progressive profiling from the start – collecting key details across multiple interactions rather than asking for everything upfront. It would have reduced abandonment further and improved data quality downstream.

↳ Revolut Connection

The Revolut JD calls out "owning data models and pipelines to and from the website" and "adding new CMS components with CRM integrations." That's exactly what this project delivered – building the data bridge between a high-traffic website and a CRM, then defining the CMS processes to scale it. The experience with Salesforce Marketing Cloud maps directly to Revolut's need for reliable data integrations that feed the Business sign-up funnel.
